The Beginning

From Humble Roots
to Royal Bhog

The story of Sattvik begins in the narrow, aromatic lanes of Old Ahmedabad. Established in 1995 by the Sharma family, it started as a modest eatery dedicated to the concept of 'Sattvic' diningâ€”food that is pure, essential, and energy-giving, much like the offerings to Lord Krishna.

Over three decades, we have evolved. What remains unchanged is our commitment to the Vaishnava philosophy. We realized that vegetarian food was often treated as a side dish in fine dining. We set out to change that narrative, proving that plant-based cuisine can be complex, luxurious, and utterly divine.

The Sharma Family

Purity (Shuddhata)

We adhere strictly to Sattvic principles. No artificial preservatives, fresh organic produce, and a balance of flavors that aids digestion and vitality.

Heritage (Parampara)

Our recipes are not invented; they are rediscovered. We scour ancient texts and royal archives to bring lost Indian culinary treasures back to your plate.

Artistry (Kala)

We eat with our eyes. Every dish is a canvas, plated with modern precision to create a visual feast that matches the depth of flavor.

The Maestro

Chef Aryan Sharma

With over 20 years of experience in Michelin-starred kitchens across London and Dubai, Chef Aryan returned to his roots to redefine Indian vegetarian cuisine for the modern festival.

"Vegetarianism is not a limitation; it is an opportunity to explore the true essence of nature. My goal is to make the vegetable the hero of the plate."

Awarded "Chef of the Year 2024" by Culinary India, Aryan brings a scientific approach to traditional spice blends, ensuring consistency and depth in every bite.

Farm To Fork

Conscious Sourcing

Great food starts in the soil. We partner directly with organic farmers in Gujarat and spice plantations in Kerala. Our dairy is sourced from free-range farms, and our saffron comes directly from the valleys of Kashmir.
